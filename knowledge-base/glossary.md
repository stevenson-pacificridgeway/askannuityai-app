# Annuity Glossary: Plain-English Definitions

- **Account value (accumulation value):** the real, spendable money in your annuity; grows with credited interest; passes to heirs.
- **Annuitization:** converting a lump sum into a stream of income payments (often irreversible).
- **Benefit base (income base / income value):** a separate figure used **only** to calculate guaranteed income; **not** a withdrawable lump sum.
- **Cap rate:** the maximum interest an FIA will credit in a period.
- **Participation rate:** the percentage of an index's gain credited to an FIA.
- **Spread (margin/asset fee):** a flat percentage subtracted from an index gain before crediting.
- **Crediting method:** the formula (e.g., annual point-to-point) translating index performance into your interest.
- **DIA (Deferred Income Annuity):** lump sum now, income starting on a future date you choose.
- **Exclusion ratio:** the portion of each non-qualified annuity payment treated as a tax-free return of principal.
- **FIA (Fixed Indexed Annuity):** principal-protected annuity that credits index-linked interest up to limits; zero in down years.
- **GLWB (Guaranteed Lifetime Withdrawal Benefit):** an income rider guaranteeing lifetime withdrawals even if the account value reaches zero.
- **Joint life:** income continues until the second of two spouses passes; pays a lower percentage than single life.
- **MYGA (Multi-Year Guaranteed Annuity):** a fixed annuity that locks a guaranteed rate for a set term; the annuity answer to a CD.
- **Period certain:** a guarantee that payments continue for at least a set number of years, even if the annuitant dies.
- **Rider:** an optional add-on (often for income or death benefit), usually for an annual fee.
- **Roll-up rate:** the guaranteed growth rate of the **benefit base** during deferral (commonly 5%–8%).
- **RMD (Required Minimum Distribution):** mandatory withdrawals from tax-deferred accounts starting at age 73 (75 in 2033).
- **SPIA (Single Premium Immediate Annuity):** lump sum now, income starts almost immediately.
- **Surrender charge:** a fee for withdrawing more than the penalty-free amount during the surrender period.
- **Sequence-of-returns risk:** the danger that poor returns early in retirement, combined with withdrawals, permanently shrink savings.
- **1035 exchange:** a tax-free transfer from one annuity (or life policy) to another annuity.
- **72(t) / SEPP:** an IRS rule allowing penalty-free pre-59½ withdrawals via substantially equal periodic payments.
