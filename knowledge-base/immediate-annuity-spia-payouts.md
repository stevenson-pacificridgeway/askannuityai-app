# Immediate Income (SPIA): Representative Payout Rates

A **Single Premium Immediate Annuity (SPIA)** converts a lump sum into a paycheck that starts right away. The older you are when you start, the higher the payout — because the expected payment period is shorter.

## Representative 2026 life-only payouts per $100,000
| Age | Monthly | Annual |
|---|---|---|
| 60 | ~$594 | ~$7,125 |
| 65 | ~$651 | ~$7,809 |
| 70 | ~$733 | ~$8,796 |

*(Surveyed mid-2026, life-only, $100,000 premium, before any state premium tax. Rates change often and vary by carrier and state.)*

## Scaling up
Payouts scale roughly with premium. For example, at age 65 a **$500,000** life-only SPIA would generate on the order of **5 × $651 ≈ $3,255/month** (~$39,000/year) — *illustrative, not a quote.*

## Single vs. joint life
Adding **joint life** (income for as long as either spouse lives) **reduces the monthly amount by roughly 12%–16%** versus life-only — you'd receive about **84%–88%** of the single-life payment. Other options (e.g., "period certain" guaranteeing payments for at least 10 or 20 years, or a cash-refund feature) also lower the payout in exchange for added protection for heirs.

## Trade-offs to teach
- **Strength:** maximum, simplest guaranteed income; takes sequence risk off the table.
- **Weakness:** typically **irreversible** and **illiquid** — once you annuitize, you generally can't get the lump sum back. That's why SPIAs usually fit only a *portion* of someone's wealth.

## When a SPIA fits
Someone who needs income **now**, values certainty over flexibility, and has other liquid assets for emergencies. It's the cleanest "pension you buy yourself."
