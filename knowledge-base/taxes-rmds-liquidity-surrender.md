# Taxes, RMDs, Liquidity, and Surrender Charges

## Tax treatment
- **Qualified (IRA/401k) annuities:** funded with pre-tax money; **all** income is taxed as **ordinary income** when received. A direct (trustee-to-trustee) **rollover** moves money in with **no tax at transfer**.
- **Non-qualified (after-tax) annuities:** only the **growth** is taxable; for income payments, an "exclusion ratio" treats part of each payment as a tax-free return of your principal.
- **Tax deferral:** inside any annuity, growth isn't taxed until withdrawn — a real advantage over yearly-taxed vehicles like CDs.
- **Roth:** qualified Roth withdrawals can be **tax-free** (subject to IRS age and holding rules).

## Required Minimum Distributions (RMDs)
Under current law, RMDs begin at **age 73** (rising to **75 in 2033**) for tax-deferred retirement accounts. A properly structured annuity can be **designed to satisfy RMDs**; income-rider payments often count toward the RMD on that contract. Coordinate specifics with a tax professional.

## The 10% early-withdrawal penalty
Withdrawing taxable gains before **age 59½** generally triggers a **10% IRS penalty** on top of ordinary income tax. Exceptions exist (for example, the **72(t)** substantially-equal-payments rule).

## Liquidity and surrender charges
Annuities are **long-term** contracts. Most allow a **penalty-free withdrawal** each year (commonly around **10%** of the value). Withdrawing **more than that during the surrender period** (often **7–10 years**, declining over time) incurs a **surrender charge**. This is precisely why a sound plan annuitizes only a **portion** of savings and keeps other money fully liquid.

## The 1035 exchange
You can move money from one annuity (or life policy) to another annuity **tax-free** via a **1035 exchange** — useful at the end of a MYGA term or to upgrade to a better contract, as long as it genuinely benefits you.

## The bottom line
Annuities reward **patience and matching the term to your timeline.** Used for money you won't need to touch in a lump sum, their tax deferral and guarantees are powerful; used for short-term cash, the surrender schedule works against you.
