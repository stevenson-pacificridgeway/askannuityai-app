# Suitability: When an Annuity Is — and Isn't — the Right Tool

The honest answer to "should I buy an annuity?" is **"it depends — and only for the right job."** Annuities solve specific problems extremely well, and are the wrong tool for others. Good education means showing both sides.

## An annuity may be appropriate when the person…
- Has an **income gap** between essential expenses and guaranteed sources (Social Security, pension);
- Is **loss-averse** and wants a portion of money **protected from market loss**;
- Worries about **outliving their money** (longevity) or a **crash early in retirement** (sequence risk);
- Wants a **paycheck they can't outlive**, especially to protect a surviving spouse;
- Has **other liquid assets** so they can commit only a **portion** to a longer-term contract;
- Values **certainty** and is comfortable trading some upside for protection.

## An annuity may NOT be appropriate when the person…
- Already covers essentials with **pension + Social Security** (little income gap to fill);
- Needs **full liquidity** or may need the lump sum back soon;
- Has a **short life expectancy** (less benefit from lifetime guarantees);
- Wants to **maximize growth** and can tolerate market swings;
- Doesn't understand the contract — **complexity that isn't explained is a red flag.**

## The suitability checklist a good professional uses
1. Does this solve a **real problem** (income gap, loss protection, longevity)?
2. Is the client keeping **enough liquid money outside** the contract?
3. Does the client understand the **surrender schedule, fees, caps/participation, and the benefit-base-vs-account-value distinction**?
4. For couples, does **joint-life** match their reliance on the income?
5. Is the **source of funds** appropriate (e.g., a direct rollover, not cashing out and triggering taxes)?

## The guiding principle
Lead with the **person's goal**, never the product. The best plans usually **guarantee the income that's needed** with one slice of savings and keep the rest invested and flexible — "invest the assets, buy the income." An annuity is a precise tool for a specific purpose, not a one-size-fits-all answer.
