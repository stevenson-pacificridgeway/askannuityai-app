# MYGAs and Safe-Money Growth (the Annuity Answer to a CD)

A **Multi-Year Guaranteed Annuity (MYGA)** locks in a single **guaranteed interest rate for a set term** (commonly 3, 5, 7, or 10 years) with **no market risk**. Think of it as the insurance industry's CD — with three differences that favor retirees.

## Why retirees often prefer a MYGA to a CD
1. **Higher rate.** In 2026, top MYGAs pay about **5.0%–5.75%** (best 5-year near **6.3%**), versus roughly **4.15%** for a top 5-year CD — typically **1–2 points more**.
2. **Tax deferral.** MYGA interest grows **tax-deferred** — you owe nothing until you withdraw. A CD's interest is **taxed every year**, even if you don't touch it. On non-IRA money, that deferral compounds into a meaningful edge over time.
3. **Backing.** A MYGA is backed by the **insurer plus state guaranty associations**; a CD is backed by the **FDIC**. Both are strong; they're just different safety nets.

## When a CD is the better choice
- You're saving a **smaller amount**, or
- You need the money **soon** (short- to mid-term goals, like a car in two years).
CDs win on **short horizons and full liquidity**; MYGAs win on **rate, tax deferral, and multi-year safe growth**.

## How a MYGA fits a retirement plan
MYGAs are ideal for the **safe, "I cannot lose this" bucket** — money earmarked for the next few years of spending, an emergency reserve, or a conservative anchor — while other dollars pursue protected growth (FIA) or guaranteed income (income rider / SPIA). At the end of the term you can renew, roll to another annuity (a tax-free 1035 exchange), or take the money.

## Watch-outs
MYGAs carry **surrender charges** if you withdraw more than the allowed penalty-free amount before the term ends, and gains withdrawn before age 59½ may face a 10% IRS penalty. Match the **term to when you'll actually need the money.**
